BRICS (BRown Interaction Capture Systems)

Lead Design Engineer for the BRICS reseach group

I have spent the last three years developing multi-camera caputre systems which records video of human interaction from dozens of view point, simutaneously, for syncronous data capture. This data can then be used to create 3D reconstructions of scenes for spatio-temporal visualization and further analysis.
The first version of our capture stage, BRICS Mini, was built around a rigid frame, and was designed to capture small, table top scenes of object-hand interaction.
Scaling up from this, we wanted to capture scenes at a room scale, rather than a hand scale, so we designed a more modular "bric" that could be mounted to the walls and ceiling of a space. This new system could now capture human-object interaction rather than hand-object interaction.
Currently, we want to move away from the confines of a room with 4 walls to make this system more mobile and generalizable. We are working on a tripod system that is fast to set up and easy to move around in order to increase the variety of captured data.
All of these designs were created, assembled, and animated utilizing the CAD software Fusion360.

HoloWorld

Designing Humanity Centered Technology, Final Project:

An affordable 2.5 Dimensional viewing experience

A simple device designed to enhance the viewing experience of portable devices. Based off of the idea of Pepper's Ghost Hologram, Holoworld is meant to bring more dimension to our 2 dimensional screens through the use of optical illusions. This project was designed to be laser cut out of cardboard and plastic with the intetion that this could be an easily accessible and widespread technology. We have published a paper explaining our product and we are currently in the process of filing a patent for our designs.

Machined Screw Plate and Screw Driver

Course in Mechanical Technology

Screw Plate : Followed detailed design schematic to fabricate a screw plate used to visualize and classify screws and screw holes. Using a CNC Milling Machine and Drill Press to measure, engrave, drill, and chamfer holes. Hand tapped the holes to make threads. And Hand polished.

Screw Driver: Forged the head of the screw driver out of a steel rod and filed it down to shape. Head treated and annealed the tip for desired strenght. For the handle, I milled flutes into a stock piece of delrin and turned it on the lathe for an ergonomic shape. Lastly, I knurled the end of the rod and press fit it into the handle for the final product.
